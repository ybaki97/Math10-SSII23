Using df.groupby and a for-loop#

Overview of our setup: We‚Äôll first go through the problem statement and talk about what the problem is asking for. We‚Äôll then break the problem into smaller pieces and better under df.groupby objects before formally writing out a solution.

GroupBy objects can be pretty mysterious. The point of these problems is to get more comfortable with them.

(There are other solutions possible, but the point of this lecture is to practice iterating over a pandas GroupBy object.)

There are 14 total industries in the dataset. Here are two examples of the sentences we want to produce. Notice how in one of the sentences we use the word ‚Äúincreased‚Äù and in the other sentence we use the word ‚Äúdecreased‚Äù. Notice also that each of our numbers shows two decimal places.

From 2002 to 2008, the median unemployment rate in Leisure and hospitality increased by 2.94%, from 8.50 to 8.75.

From 2002 to 2008, the median unemployment rate in Manufacturing decreased by 18.18%, from 6.60 to 5.40.

Note: I‚Äôm not worried about the sign of this number. We‚Äôll take care of it when we code the solution to the problem. Formula for percent change: $\( \frac{x_{\text{new}}-x_{\text{old}}}{x_{\text{old}}} \times 100 \)$
